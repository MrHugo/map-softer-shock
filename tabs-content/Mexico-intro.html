HEALTH SECTOR

INDUSTRIAL PROJECTS

In October 2009, Mexican researchers and companies directors discussed about the future development of applied synthetic biology, reaching two main conclusions. First, Mexico is is relatively late in examining these issues. Secondly, the research directions that would allow Mexico to be competitive were not clearly defined. http://www.biosintetica.mx

Despite this, Mexico has made important contributions to synthetic biology.

In 1980, the Mexican scientist Francisco Bolivar Zapata was able to access the DNA of a pancreatic cell and from there obtained the gene that encodes insulin.
